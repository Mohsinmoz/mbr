RESEARCH

The Mohammed bin Rashid Centre for Government is pleased to announce the launch of an initiative aimed at enhancing public sector service delivery in the Arab region.

Purpose

Citizen-centric services is at the forefront of priorities for governments worldwide. Despite the regional and international focus, research in this area is limited to assessments made by consultants working in the field. In light of this limitation of evidence based field research that is context specific to the Arab World , Mohammed bin Rashid Centre is launching a multi-disciplinary research grant initiative, and is inviting interested researchers to submit their proposals.

Funding

Maximum of U.S. $ 10,000 funding per research that is spread over a maximum period of a year.

Application Process

Interested researchers are requested to send a copy of their research proposal and a cover letter clearly outlining their contact details to research@mbrcg.aeThe proposal document should include:

  • Abstract

    The abstract should clearly outline – in no more than 250 words - the hypothesis that the research aims to address and how it will contribute to service delivery in the region. It should also highlight the proposed research methodology and its duration.

  • Project Proposal

    The project proposal should detail the research objectives, questions and expected outcomes. It should also elaborate on the proposed methodology and the timeline – indicating the key milestones and the research team. Importantly, the proposal should include a budget - with a narrative justification of expenses where needed.

The project proposal should not exceed 15 double spaced pages, in font size no smaller than 11.

For more information, questions or comments on the "improving government services in the Arab world", please email research@mbrcg.ae

Arab countries include Algeria, Bahrain, Comoros, Djibouti, Egypt, Iraq, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Libya, Palestine, Mauritania, Morocco, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Somalia, Sudan, Syria, Tunisa, United Arab Emirates, Yemen

About the Centre

Mohammed bin Rashid Centre for Government

Led by the vision of His Highness Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, UAE Vice President and Prime Minister and Ruler of Dubai, the Mohammed bin Rashid Centre for Government is an online “one stop shop” for public sector practitioners and academics interested in public policy issues in the Arab world.

The Centre is guided by His Highness vision for citizen-centric, modern government across the region. Realizing the strong need for tailored resources and material on Arab government, the Centre aims at building and strengthening capacity in Arab public administration through knowledge production and dissemination, capacity building through the provision of online tools and resources through an online platform.
